Regression Results Explained

The analysis estimates the causal effect of historical exposure to the mining mita using a geographic Regression Discontinuity design at the mita boundary.

  • Mita exposure reduces household consumption by approximately 25%.
  • Mita exposure increases child stunting by around 6 percentage points.
  • Results are robust across alternative RD specifications (cubic in latitude/longitude, distance to Potosí, and distance to mita boundary).

These findings reflect long-run institutional persistence operating through land tenure systems and public goods provision.
